# STEM Reasoning Prompt Dataset for LLM Evaluation

This dataset was created to evaluate the reasoning abilities of large language models (LLMs) on analytical problems in mathematics, logic, and scientific explanation.

The prompts are designed to test:
- multi-step mathematical reasoning
- logical inference
- conceptual understanding
- explanation clarity

---

## Prompt 1 – Multi-Step Arithmetic Reasoning
A car travels 60 miles at 60 mph and then another 60 miles at 30 mph.
What is the average speed for the entire trip?
Explain your reasoning step by step.

---

## Prompt 2 – Logical Inference
All squares are rectangles.  
All rectangles are quadrilaterals.  
Some quadrilaterals are not squares.

Is the statement **“Some rectangles are not squares”** logically guaranteed by these premises?  
Explain your reasoning.

---

## Prompt 3 – Algebraic Problem Solving
A farmer has chickens and cows.  
There are 30 heads and 84 legs in total.

How many chickens and how many cows are there?  
Show your reasoning.

---

## Prompt 4 – Conceptual Physics Understanding
Explain the difference between **speed** and **velocity** using a real-world example.

---

## Prompt 5 – Scientific Reasoning
Why does **correlation not imply causation**?  
Provide a real-world example where two variables are correlated but neither causes the other.

---

## Prompt 6 – Estimation and Assumptions
Estimate how many piano tuners might exist in a large city.  
Describe the assumptions used in your reasoning.

---

## Prompt 7 – Mathematical Explanation
Explain why the derivative of \(x^2\) is \(2x\).  
Provide an intuitive explanation rather than only stating the rule.

---

## Prompt 8 – Error Detection
A student claims that the average of 10 and 100 is 55 and therefore the average speed of traveling equal distances at 10 mph and 100 mph is also 55 mph.  
Explain why this reasoning is incorrect.
